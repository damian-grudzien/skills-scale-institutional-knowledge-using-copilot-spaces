# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

---

## Quality Assurance Engineers

### Role Summary
QA Engineers ensure product quality through systematic testing, defect identification, and validation of acceptance criteria. They work closely with developers and product managers to prevent issues before they reach production.

### Responsibilities
- Design and execute test plans and test cases
- Identify, document, and track defects through resolution
- Validate that features meet acceptance criteria and quality standards
- Collaborate on test automation strategies
- Provide quality metrics and trend reporting

### Goals
- Minimize defects reaching production
- Reduce cycle time for quality validation
- Enable rapid, confident releases

### Typical Communication
- Test planning and review sessions with developers
- Defect reports and quality metrics dashboards
- Acceptance criteria validation with product managers

### Key Interactions
- **Developers**: Participate in requirements clarification, review test results, address identified defects
- **Product Managers**: Validate feature specifications align with acceptance criteria
- **Project Managers**: Report on quality status and blockers

---

## Technical Architects

### Role Summary
Technical Architects design scalable, maintainable solutions that meet both current and future business requirements. They provide technical leadership and ensure architectural consistency across projects.

### Responsibilities
- Design system architecture and technical solutions
- Review and guide major technical decisions
- Identify technical risks and propose mitigation strategies
- Mentor developers on architectural patterns and best practices
- Ensure compliance with security, performance, and scalability standards

### Goals
- Build systems that scale with business growth
- Reduce technical debt and maintenance costs
- Enable team autonomy through clear architectural guidance

### Typical Communication
- Design review sessions and architecture decision records (ADRs)
- Technical mentoring and code review guidance
- Risk assessment and technical feasibility analysis

### Key Interactions
- **Developers**: Provide technical guidance, conduct design reviews, support implementation
- **Project Managers**: Advise on technical feasibility and timeline implications
- **Product Managers**: Translate business requirements into technical solutions

---

## Scrum Masters / Agile Coaches

### Role Summary
Scrum Masters facilitate agile ceremonies, remove impediments, and coach teams on agile practices. They enable continuous improvement and sustainable delivery pace.

### Responsibilities
- Facilitate daily standups, sprint planning, reviews, and retrospectives
- Identify and help resolve team impediments and blockers
- Coach team members on agile practices and mindset
- Track sprint metrics and team velocity
- Ensure team follows agile principles and processes

### Goals
- Maximize team velocity and predictability
- Foster a culture of continuous improvement
- Enable self-organizing, high-performing teams

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint ceremonies
- Retrospective notes and action items
- Velocity reports and process improvement suggestions

### Key Interactions
- **Project Managers**: Align on timeline and scope management; support stakeholder communication
- **All Roles**: Remove blockers, facilitate collaboration, model agile principles

---

## Stakeholder Managers / Business Analysts

### Role Summary
Stakeholder Managers ensure alignment between project delivery and business objectives. They gather requirements, manage expectations, and serve as the bridge between business and delivery teams.

### Responsibilities
- Gather and clarify business requirements
- Document use cases, user stories, and acceptance criteria
- Manage stakeholder expectations and communications
- Identify and communicate business risks and constraints
- Validate delivered solutions meet business needs

### Goals
- Ensure projects deliver measurable business value
- Minimize scope creep and misalignment
- Build strong stakeholder relationships and trust

### Typical Communication
- Requirements workshops and stakeholder interviews
- User story documentation and acceptance criteria specs
- Status updates and change impact assessments

### Key Interactions
- **Product Managers**: Collaborate on prioritization and product vision
- **Project Managers**: Communicate status and manage expectations
- **Developers/QA**: Clarify requirements and validate solutions

## How these personas are used in the exercise
- Use these persona definitions to frame scenarios and sample interactions in the Skills Exercise.
- Each persona can be used as a persona prompt for Copilot Spaces to shape role-specific guidance.
- When assigning tasks or responsibilities, reference the relevant personas to ensure clarity and accountability.
- Use persona interactions to identify collaboration points and potential communication gaps.
